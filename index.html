Welcome to my portfolio!

My love for electronics started at the age of 7. I would take apart radio-controlled cars and attempt to figure out how they worked. By 14 years old, I had built my first computer, was a part of my school's electronics club, and was a participant of the College Now program offered by my High School. Because of my dedication and love for electronics, I obtained a High School Diploma with honors and college credits.

I continued my post-secondary studies by partaking in a work-study scholarship with the Republic of Korea's National Institute for International Education (TaLK division). This wonderful opportunity allowed me to work towards earning my Bachelor of Science Degree while traveling the world and teaching underprivileged, rural students English.

Today, I use what I've learned through obtaining my ABET-accredited Bachelor of Science Degree in Computer Engineering Technology to build electronics, code programs, and make websites. I’m currently in a Full-stack Development certificate course with Code-Louisville, and am studying to take a GRE so I can pursue a Master of Science Degree. I’m dedicated to learning all things technology that interact with the world, and welcome any opportunity that allows me to strengthen my skills and use my acquired knowledge.

  • 1st Front End Attempt

    Obsolete

    The challenges I took with my first attempt at Front End Web Development was to: make it Responsive, Create Custom CSS, Utilize Responsive CSS, use Flexbox and Grid CSS, Establish Interactive Responses with JavaScript, Demonstrate Excellent Comment Utilization, use DevTools to Debug, and Implement Front End Form.

    The site is responsive to the screen size of the device viewing the page, which I implemented through css and JavaScript. One of the responsiveness reatures I practiced with was responsive columns and grid layout. When its viewed in larger screens, it'll be a column layout. medium screens will have a downward cascading grid that expands to improve readability. On small screens it becomes rows which can expand out to display the information. The interactive portions of my landing page uses JavaScript to execute. I've created a more JavaScript/JQuery app which has more focus on interactivity. Follow the link to the 'Color Game' to try it out! If you's like to see my excellent use of comments, check out my Github source files.

  • JavaScript Color Game

    Work In Progress

    Practicing JavaScript, or even implementing it on you webpage is a great way to sharpen your skills with it. But why not create something you'll want to return to and even challenge your friends?

    The challenge of this game is to spot the cell which has a different shade than the rest, upon selecting the correct one, it'll change spots and decrease the difference in its shade from the others. Eventually you'll get to a point where the random cell is equal to the others, at which point, YOU WIN! Go ahead and give my Color Game a try!
